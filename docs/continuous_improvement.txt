Continuous Improvement is a management philosophy focused on making small, ongoing improvements to processes, products, and services over time. Instead of relying only on large transformations, organizations pursue incremental improvements that gradually enhance efficiency, quality, and productivity.

The concept of continuous improvement is strongly associated with frameworks such as Lean, Six Sigma, and Kaizen. These approaches encourage organizations to constantly evaluate their processes, identify inefficiencies, and implement improvements.

One commonly used continuous improvement cycle is the PDCA cycle:

Plan – Identify an opportunity for improvement and plan a change.
Do – Implement the change on a small scale.
Check – Measure the results and evaluate whether the change improved performance.
Act – Standardize the improvement if successful or adjust the plan if necessary.

Continuous improvement encourages employee involvement, collaboration, and data-driven decision making. Employees at all levels are encouraged to suggest improvements and identify process inefficiencies.

Organizations that adopt continuous improvement practices often achieve better operational efficiency, reduced costs, improved product quality, and higher customer satisfaction.
