Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are measurable values that organizations use to evaluate how effectively they are achieving key objectives. KPIs provide a way to track progress and measure success across projects, teams, and business initiatives.

Effective KPI tracking involves defining clear targets, collecting reliable data, and reviewing performance regularly. KPIs should be aligned with strategic business goals so that progress toward those goals can be measured objectively.

Examples of common KPI categories include operational efficiency, financial performance, customer satisfaction, quality metrics, and process improvement outcomes.

KPI dashboards are often used to visualize performance metrics. These dashboards help managers quickly identify trends, detect underperforming initiatives, and make informed decisions.

Organizations typically monitor KPI achievement levels to determine whether initiatives are meeting expectations or require corrective action.
